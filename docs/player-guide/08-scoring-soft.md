# Scoring — Soft Scores & Voting

Beyond battle results, many tournaments include soft scores — painting, sportsmanship, hobby achievements — and voting categories like Best Painted Army or Favourite Opponent. These appear as separate cards in the Battle Hub after your match.

![Soft scores card](./08-scoring-soft.png)

## Soft Scores

If the tournament has soft score categories, a **Soft Scores** card appears below your match card (with a star icon). Soft scores are submitted separately from battle results and don't require opponent confirmation.

### Two Types of Soft Scores

The soft score form is divided into two sections:

![Soft score input](./08-scoring-soft-input.png)

#### Rate Your Opponent

Categories where you score your opponent — typically sportsmanship, painting quality, or list presentation. Your rating is recorded on your **opponent's** record (not yours).

For example, if you rate your opponent's painting as 4 out of 5, that score goes toward their painting total.

#### Self Assessment

Categories where you score yourself — for example, a hobby checklist or self-reported painting level. Your rating is recorded on **your own** record.

### Input Methods

Each category may use one of two input methods:

| Method | How it works |
|--------|-------------|
| **Numeric** | A single number input with a range — e.g., "Sportsmanship (1-5)". Enter a value within the allowed range. |
| **Rubric** | A checklist of criteria, each worth set points. Score each criterion individually. The total is calculated automatically. Quick-action links **All** (max all criteria) and **Clear** (zero all criteria) appear at the top right. |

### Submitting Soft Scores

Once you've filled in all categories, click **Submit Scores**. A green checkmark and the message "Soft Scores Submitted for this round" confirms your submission.

**Important:** Soft scores are a one-time submission per round. You cannot edit them after submitting, so double-check your entries before clicking submit.

## Voting

If the tournament includes voting categories (Best Painted Army, Player's Choice, Favourite Opponent, etc.), a separate **Cast Your Vote** card appears with a vote icon.

![Voting interface](./08-scoring-soft-voting.png)

### How Voting Works

For each voting category, you'll see a dropdown of eligible players. Select the player you want to vote for.

**Vote filtering:**
- If the category is set to "opponents only", you'll only see players you've faced during the tournament
- Self-voting is excluded unless the TO has specifically allowed it
- You cannot vote for the same player twice in a multi-vote category

### Multi-Vote (Ranked Voting)

Some categories allow multiple ranked votes — for example, 1st place (3 points), 2nd place (2 points), and 3rd place (1 point). Each rank has its own dropdown. You must select a different player for each rank.

The ordinal labels (1st vote, 2nd vote, 3rd vote) and point values are shown next to each dropdown.

### Submitting Votes

Click **Submit Votes** when you've made your selections. A green checkmark and "Votes Submitted" confirms your submission.

**Important:** Like soft scores, votes are a one-time submission. You cannot change them after submitting.

## When Do These Appear?

Soft scores and voting cards are only visible when:
- The current round is **active** or in **scoring** state
- You're checked in
- The tournament has soft score or voting categories configured
- The categories are active for the current round (TOs can restrict categories to specific rounds)
