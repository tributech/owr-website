# Pairings & Your Match

Once the TO posts pairings for a round, you can find your table assignment and opponent in the Battle Hub.

![Pairings list](./05-pairings.png)

## Pairings Tab

The **Pairings** tab shows every matchup for the current round as a compact list. Each pairing displays:

- **Table number** — e.g., "T5" — so you know where to sit
- **Player 1** — Name and faction
- **Player 2** — Name and faction
- **Score** — VP and outcome badges once the match is scored (or "vs" if still in progress)

Your own match is highlighted so it's easy to spot in the list. Outcome badges use colour coding: green for a win, red for a loss, yellow for a draw.

If pairings haven't been posted yet, you'll see a message: "Pairings not yet posted".

## Match Tab

The **Match** tab focuses on your specific matchup. It shows a card with:

- **Round number and scenario** — e.g., "Round 2 — Dawn Attack"
- **Table number** — Displayed prominently as a badge
- **Start time** — If the round is in `Locked` state and has a scheduled start time
- **Your player card** — Labelled "YOU" with your name, faction, and army list link
- **Opponent's player card** — Labelled "OPPONENT" with their name, faction, and army list link

From here you can tap on your opponent's army list to review it before the game.

## Bye Matches

If you have a bye (no opponent due to odd player count), the Match tab shows a **Bye Match** card. You receive automatic tournament points for the round — typically equivalent to a draw. No scoring is needed from you.

## Doubles & Teams

For doubles tournaments, each player card also shows:

- **Team name** — Your team's name
- **Partner** — Your partner's name and faction

Both partners share the same match result and score.

## Awaiting Pairings

If pairings haven't been posted for the current round, you'll see an **Awaiting Pairings** card with a message letting you know to sit tight. If table or scenario information is already set, it may appear here as a preview.
